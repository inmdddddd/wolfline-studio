Who Is Aether

Born of Darkness, Keeper of Light

In the beginning, before Olympus and the gods who would come to rule it, Chaos gave rise to Erebus — the primordial Darkness — and Nyx, the primordial Night. From their union came two children: Hemera, the Day, and Aether, the shining upper air.

Where Hemera governed the coming and going of daylight upon the mortal earth, Aether ruled the bright, rarefied air above it: the atmosphere breathed only by the immortals, older than Olympus itself.

A paradox at the root of creation — pure light, born from the deepest dark.
The Realm Above Olympus

Beyond cloud. Beyond storm. Beyond the reach of mortal breath. The realm of Aether exists beyond cloud, storm, and mortal breath — where the turbid air of earth gives way to a clear, radiant firmament the ancients believed clothed the heavens in permanent light.

Not a place the gods visit. The air they are made of.
